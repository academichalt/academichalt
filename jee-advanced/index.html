Best Books for JEE Advanced — IIT Preparation

JEE Advanced is considered one of the most difficult undergraduate entrance exams in the world. Only the top 2.5 lakh students from JEE Main are eligible to appear, and only the top ~17,000 make it to the IITs. The books required for JEE Advanced go significantly beyond NCERT and require deep conceptual understanding and problem-solving ability.

JEE Advanced Physics Books

HC Verma's Concepts of Physics (Volume 1 and 2) is the undisputed bible for JEE Advanced Physics. Every serious IIT aspirant must solve this book completely — all exercises, worked examples, and even the conceptual questions. After HC Verma, IE Irodov (Problems in General Physics) is used for the most challenging problems. DC Pandey's Electricity and Magnetism book is also excellent for that topic specifically.

JEE Advanced Chemistry Books

For Organic Chemistry, MS Chouhan's book on Organic Chemistry is the gold standard for JEE Advanced. For Physical Chemistry, Narendra Awasthi is widely recommended. For Inorganic Chemistry, JD Lee is the comprehensive reference alongside NCERT. VK Jaiswal's Inorganic Chemistry is another popular choice.

JEE Advanced Mathematics Books

SL Loney (Trigonometry and Coordinate Geometry), Hall and Knight (Higher Algebra), and GN Berman (Mathematical Analysis) are classic references for JEE Advanced Maths. Vikas Gupta and Pankaj Joshi's books from Black Book series are also highly recommended by recent toppers.
