About This Book

HC Verma's Concepts of Physics is widely considered the most important book for JEE Advanced Physics preparation. Written by Professor H.C. Verma of IIT Kanpur, the book is famous for its lucid explanations, intuitive approach to problem-solving, and rigorous exercise sets that build conceptual depth.

Volume 1 covers Mechanics (Kinematics, Dynamics, Rotational Motion, Gravitation), Properties of Matter, Waves, and Thermodynamics. Volume 2 covers Electricity, Magnetism, Optics, Modern Physics, and Nuclear Physics. Together, they cover the complete JEE Advanced Physics syllabus.

Who Should Read This Book

  • JEE Advanced aspirants — This is the #1 book for IIT preparation. Every serious JEE Advanced student must solve this completely.
  • JEE Main students — Solving HC Verma will put you well ahead for JEE Main Physics too.
  • Class 11 & 12 students — Build exceptionally strong conceptual understanding from the very beginning.
  • NEET aspirants — Chapter-end exercises are useful for NEET Physics though the difficulty is higher than needed.

How This Book Helps in Exams

HC Verma builds intuition — not just formula memorisation. Every JEE Advanced Physics problem tests whether you truly understand the concept, not just whether you can apply a formula. The worked examples in HC Verma teach you to think through problems step by step. The chapter-end exercises are graded — starting from direct application and progressing to multi-concept problems that appear in JEE Advanced.

IIT toppers from the last decade consistently list HC Verma as the most important book they solved. Many top-100 rankers mention solving HC Verma 3–4 times over their preparation period.

Frequently Asked Questions

HC Verma is the single most important book for JEE Advanced Physics. It covers all concepts with great depth and its exercises are at the level of JEE Advanced. However, for the most challenging multi-concept problems and integer-type questions, you can also supplement with DC Pandey's advanced exercises or past JEE papers after completing HC Verma.
Many students prefer to read NCERT first to get the basic framework, then move to HC Verma for deeper understanding. However, HC Verma itself is written with enough clarity that many students read it first and then refer to NCERT for exam-oriented questions. Either approach works. For board exams, NCERT is mandatory.
Top JEE Advanced rankers typically solve HC Verma 2–3 times. The first reading is for concept building and solving exercises with the help of solutions. The second reading focuses on solving exercises independently. The third pass focuses on the hardest problems and objective exercises. Each pass deepens your understanding significantly.
HC Verma is more advanced than what NEET requires, but reading the theory sections builds very strong concepts that help in NEET. NEET aspirants can read the theory from HC Verma but practice MCQs from NEET-specific books like DC Pandey Objective Physics or MTG books, which have questions calibrated to the NEET difficulty level.
