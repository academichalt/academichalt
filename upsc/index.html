🏛️ UPSC Books — Free PDF Download

Standard books recommended by IAS toppers and UPSC coaching institutes — free to download for every aspirant.

Best Books for UPSC IAS Prelims and Mains 2025

The UPSC Civil Services Examination (IAS exam) is the most prestigious competitive examination in India. It consists of three stages: Preliminary Examination (Prelims), Main Examination (Mains), and Personality Test (Interview). Preparing for UPSC requires a thorough understanding of a vast and diverse syllabus spanning multiple subjects.

UPSC Indian Polity Books

M. Laxmikanth's Indian Polity is the single most important book for UPSC Polity and Governance. It is comprehensive, clearly written, and covers every aspect of the Indian Constitution, Parliament, Judiciary, Elections, and more. This is non-negotiable for every UPSC aspirant — both Prelims and Mains.

UPSC History Books

For Ancient and Medieval History, NCERT Class 6–12 History books form the foundation. RS Sharma (Ancient India) and Satish Chandra (Medieval India) provide deeper understanding. For Modern History, Spectrum Modern India by Rajiv Ahir is the most popular book among aspirants and toppers alike. It covers the colonial period, the freedom struggle, and post-1947 India in comprehensive detail.

UPSC Geography Books

GC Leong's Certificate Physical and Human Geography is essential for Physical Geography. NCERT Geography books from Class 6 to 12 cover Indian Geography comprehensively. Atlas — Oxford Student Atlas for India — is indispensable for map-based questions which appear frequently in Prelims.

UPSC Economics Books

Ramesh Singh's Indian Economy is the standard reference for UPSC Economics. The Economic Survey (published annually by the Ministry of Finance) is essential for current economic data and government policies. NCERT Class 11 and 12 Economics provide the conceptual foundation.

UPSC Science and Technology

NCERT Science books from Class 6–10 cover the basic Science and Technology topics. For advanced S&T, current affairs from reliable monthly magazines and PIB (Press Information Bureau) are the best resources. Vision IAS and InsightsIAS study materials are also widely used by toppers.
