Best Books for JEE Main 2025 — Complete Booklist for Physics, Chemistry & Mathematics

The Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) Main is the most attempted engineering entrance exam in India, with over 11 lakh students appearing each year. Getting a good rank in JEE Main requires a smart preparation strategy, and choosing the right books is the foundation of that strategy.

This article provides a carefully researched list of the best books for JEE Main 2025, based on recommendations from IIT toppers, JEE counsellors, and experienced faculty members from leading coaching institutes.

The Golden Rule: NCERT First, Always

Before jumping to reference books, understand this: approximately 60–65% of JEE Main questions are NCERT-based, especially in Chemistry. The NCERT textbooks for Class 11 and 12 (Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics) must be read thoroughly, with all exercises solved, before you open any reference book. This is not optional — it is the foundation.

Once you have a strong command of NCERT, reference books help you develop problem-solving speed, handle tricky variations, and prepare for questions that test deeper conceptual understanding.

Best Books for JEE Main Physics

Physics is often the differentiator in JEE Main — students who master Physics tend to have a significant edge in ranking. Here are the best books:

  • NCERT Physics Class 11 & 12 (Mandatory) — Read cover to cover. Solve all NCERT examples and exercises.
  • DC Pandey Physics (Arihant Series) — The most popular JEE Main Physics book. Excellent theory, concept-based examples, and a large bank of MCQs organized by topic. Available in separate volumes for Mechanics, Waves & Thermodynamics, Optics & Modern Physics, and Electricity & Magnetism.
  • H.C. Verma Concepts of Physics Vol 1 & 2 — While primarily aimed at JEE Advanced, Chapter 1–20 of HC Verma are extremely useful for JEE Main concept building too. The exercises are harder than JEE Main but solving them creates a safety margin.
  • Errorless Physics (Universal) — A large collection of topicwise MCQs sorted by difficulty. Excellent for practice once you've completed theory from DC Pandey or HC Verma.

Recommended approach: NCERT → DC Pandey Theory → DC Pandey Exercises → Previous year JEE Main papers topicwise

Best Books for JEE Main Chemistry

Chemistry is divided into three parts — Physical, Organic, and Inorganic — each requiring a slightly different approach and different books.

  • NCERT Chemistry Class 11 & 12 (Mandatory for all three parts) — For Inorganic Chemistry especially, NCERT is almost entirely sufficient for JEE Main. Read every line, every exception, every footnote.
  • Physical Chemistry — P. Bahadur (G.R. Bathla Publications) — The standard reference for numerical Physical Chemistry. Extensive practice problems organized by topic with solutions.
  • Organic Chemistry — OP Tandon (G.R. Bathla) — Excellent theory for Organic Chemistry. Covers mechanisms, reactions, and named reactions systematically. Good for building conceptual understanding before solving MCQs.
  • Organic Chemistry — Morrison & Boyd — A more advanced alternative to OP Tandon, this book explains mechanisms intuitively. Preferred by students who want deeper Organic Chemistry understanding.
  • Inorganic Chemistry — VK Jaiswal (Cengage) — For students who want extra practice in Inorganic beyond NCERT, VK Jaiswal provides topicwise MCQs with detailed solutions.

Recommended approach: NCERT (all three parts) → OP Tandon for Organic theory → P. Bahadur for Physical numericals → NCERT for Inorganic (sufficient for JEE Main)

Best Books for JEE Main Mathematics

Mathematics is typically where students invest the most time, and for good reason — it requires both conceptual clarity and calculation speed. Here are the best books:

  • NCERT Mathematics Class 11 & 12 (Foundation) — All concepts tested in JEE Main have their roots in NCERT. Solve all exercises and examples thoroughly.
  • Cengage Mathematics Series (G. Tewani) — The most comprehensive Mathematics series for JEE Main. Available in separate books for Algebra, Trigonometry, Coordinate Geometry, Calculus, Vectors, and 3D Geometry. Excellent theory with graded problems from easy to JEE-level.
  • Arihant Mathematics by Amit M. Agarwal — Another excellent series for JEE Mathematics. Many students prefer Arihant for its well-organized problem sets and detailed solutions.
  • RD Sharma Class 11 & 12 — Good for building the foundation before moving to Cengage. RD Sharma has a large number of problems at the NCERT+ level which build speed and confidence.
  • Skills in Mathematics by S.K. Goyal — Particularly excellent for Algebra and Coordinate Geometry. Contains challenging problems that appear in JEE Main.

Recommended approach: NCERT → RD Sharma for foundation → Cengage for JEE-level problems → Previous year papers topicwise

Previous Year Papers — The Non-Negotiable

Solving previous year JEE Main papers is arguably the most important part of preparation. It gives you:

  • Familiarity with the exact question types and difficulty level
  • Understanding of important topics and their weightage
  • Exam practice under time pressure
  • Confidence from solving actual exam questions

Disha Publications and Arihant both publish comprehensive collections of previous year JEE Main papers chapterwise. Solving 10 years of previous papers is mandatory for any serious JEE Main aspirant.

How to Use These Books Effectively

Having the right books is only half the equation — how you study them matters equally. Here are some tips:

  • Complete one book fully before moving to the next. Jumping between books creates gaps and confusion.
  • Maintain an error notebook. Note down every mistake you make, the concept behind it, and the correct approach. Review this notebook weekly.
  • Solve, don't read. Maths and Physics must be practised, not just read. Attempt every exercise before looking at the solution.
  • Take full mock tests weekly after completing 60% of your syllabus. Analyze your performance in detail after each test.
  • Revise NCERT monthly. Even after finishing reference books, come back to NCERT for regular revision — especially Chemistry.
