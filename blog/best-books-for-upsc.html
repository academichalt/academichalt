Essential Books for UPSC IAS Prelims & Mains 2025

The UPSC Civil Services Examination is the most prestigious and comprehensive competitive examination in India. It consists of three stages — Preliminary Examination, Main Examination, and the Personality Test (Interview). The Prelims tests General Studies and CSAT, while the Mains includes nine papers covering Essay, General Studies (4 papers), Optional Subject (2 papers), and languages.

With a vast and diverse syllabus, choosing the right books is crucial. This article presents a carefully curated reading list based on recommendations from recent IAS toppers (including AIR 1 holders from recent years), senior UPSC faculty, and Academic Halt's editorial team.

The NCERT Foundation

Before any standard reference book, every UPSC aspirant must read NCERT books from Class 6 to 12 for History, Geography, Political Science, Economics, and Science. These books build the conceptual vocabulary that helps you understand advanced references. NCERTs are also directly tested in UPSC Prelims. Start your preparation by reading all relevant NCERTs before picking up any coaching material or standard reference.

Indian Polity — Best Books

  • Indian Polity by M. Laxmikanth — The undisputed reference for UPSC Polity. This book is comprehensive, clearly organised, and directly maps to the UPSC syllabus. Every IAS aspirant must read Laxmikanth at least twice — once for understanding, once for revision. All chapters on Constitutional History, Fundamental Rights, Parliament, President, Judiciary, State Government, Local Bodies, and Constitutional Bodies are essential.
  • NCERT Class 9–12 Political Science — Read all four years of NCERT Political Science as the foundation before Laxmikanth.

History — Best Books for UPSC

  • Ancient India — NCERT Class 11 (RS Sharma)
  • Medieval India — NCERT Class 11 (Satish Chandra)
  • Modern India — Spectrum by Rajiv Ahir — The most popular book for Modern India. Covers the entire colonial period, the freedom struggle, and early post-independence India in a well-structured format accessible for both Prelims MCQs and Mains answers.
  • India's Struggle for Independence — Bipan Chandra — For deeper understanding of the freedom movement, especially for Mains.
  • Art and Culture — Nitin Singhania — Essential for the Art and Culture component which now has significant weightage in both Prelims and Mains.

Geography — Best Books for UPSC

  • Certificate Physical and Human Geography — GC Leong — Essential for Physical Geography. Covers climatology, geomorphology, oceanography, and human geography comprehensively.
  • NCERT Class 11–12 Geography (All four books) — Fundamentals of Physical Geography, India — Physical Environment, Human Geography, and India — People and Economy.
  • Oxford Student Atlas for India — Indispensable for map-based questions which regularly appear in UPSC Prelims.

Economics — Best Books

  • Indian Economy — Ramesh Singh — The most commonly recommended Economics book for UPSC. Covers all economic concepts, policies, and institutions relevant to the exam.
  • Economic Survey (Latest) — Published annually by the Ministry of Finance. Essential for current economic data, government schemes, and policy analysis. Must be read every year.
  • NCERT Economics Class 11 & 12 — Read before Ramesh Singh for conceptual foundation.

Science & Technology

NCERT Science from Class 6 to 10 covers the basic S&T topics. For advanced S&T topics like Space Technology, Biotechnology, Defence Technology, and Digital India, rely on PIB (Press Information Bureau) releases, monthly current affairs magazines, and Vision IAS / InsightsIAS study materials. The India Year Book (published by Government of India) is also a useful reference.

Current Affairs — The X Factor

UPSC increasingly tests current affairs in both Prelims and Mains. Reliable sources include: The Hindu newspaper (mandatory daily reading), Yojana and Kurukshetra magazines (government schemes and rural development), Economic and Political Weekly (EPW) for advanced economic analysis, and monthly current affairs compilations from reputed UPSC coaching institutes.
