NEET Biology Preparation Strategy 2025: How to Score 360/360

✍️ Dr. Kavita Nair📅 November 25, 2024⏱️ 9 min read👁️ 1,12,780 views
🧬 Biology = 360 marks in NEET (90 questions × 4 marks). This is the single most important subject for NEET — it accounts for 50% of total marks and is the primary differentiator between top rankers.

Biology in NEET is the gold mine. While Chemistry and Physics require problem-solving ability, Biology is largely factual and memory-based — making it possible to score a near-perfect 355-360 with the right strategy. Here's everything you need to know about NEET Biology preparation for 2025.

NEET Biology: Subject Overview

NEET Biology is divided into two parts:

🌿 Botany (Class 11 + 12)

~45 questions, ~180 marks. Covers plant biology, reproduction, genetics, ecology, and biotechnology.

🐾 Zoology (Class 11 + 12)

~45 questions, ~180 marks. Covers animal biology, human physiology, reproduction, and evolution.

The NCERT Bible Strategy

NEET Biology is 99% NCERT. Unlike JEE where you need reference books, NEET Biology questions come almost exclusively from NCERT Class 11 and 12 Biology textbooks. Here's how to master NCERT for NEET:

Step 1: Read NCERT Line-by-Line (Not Just Headings)

Every sentence in NCERT Biology matters. NEET setters pick specific lines, numbers, and diagrams directly from NCERT. Read every line, including the boxes and footnotes. Read the same chapter minimum 5-6 times.

Step 2: Master All NCERT Diagrams

Diagrams are extremely important in NEET Biology. Every year, 8-10 questions are directly based on NCERT diagrams. Draw and redraw: cell structure, heart anatomy, nephron, embryo development, meiosis stages, recombinant DNA technology flow charts.

Step 3: NCERT Exemplar for Practice

After completing NCERT textbooks, solve NCERT Exemplar problems. These are slightly higher difficulty and cover edge cases that NEET often tests.

Step 4: Make Flowcharts & Mnemonics

Biology involves vast amounts of information. Use flowcharts for processes (photosynthesis, respiration, DNA replication) and mnemonics for classification, orders, and sequences. Your brain retains visual information 65% better than text.

Chapter-wise Tips for High-Weightage Topics

Human Physiology (8–10 questions)

The single most important chapter in NEET Biology. Master: Digestion & absorption (enzymes, hormones), Breathing & gas exchange (lung volumes, partial pressures), Blood & circulation (cardiac cycle, ECG, blood pressure), Excretion (uriniferous tubule, countercurrent mechanism), Neural control (nerve impulse, synapse, reflex arc), Chemical coordination (all hormones and their functions).

Genetics & Evolution (7–9 questions)

Genetics tests your ability to apply Mendelian laws and molecular biology concepts. Key focus areas: Mendelism, sex determination, linkage and crossing over, DNA structure and replication, transcription and translation, mutations, Hardy-Weinberg principle, and evidences for evolution.

Reproduction (7–8 questions)

Covers both plant and animal reproduction. From NCERT: Flower structure and double fertilization (Botany), Female and male reproductive systems, Gametogenesis, Embryo development stages, Reproductive health and contraception.

NEET Biology Mock Test Strategy

After completing the syllabus (by Month 9-10), start intensive mock testing:

  • Take chapter-wise tests first to identify weak chapters
  • Then full-length NEET mock tests (180 questions, 3 hours)
  • Analyze every wrong answer — understand the concept, not just the answer
  • Maintain an "error notebook" — write down every wrong concept once
  • Aim for 350+ in Biology mock tests consistently before the real exam
