Best Books for NEET 2025 — Biology, Physics & Chemistry

NEET (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test) is the most competitive medical entrance examination in India, with over 20 lakh students competing for approximately 1 lakh MBBS seats across government and private medical colleges. With such fierce competition, the choice of study material can literally determine whether you become a doctor.

This article presents the most comprehensive and up-to-date booklist for NEET 2025, based on recommendations from students who cracked NEET with top ranks, faculty from leading NEET coaching institutes, and our editorial team's extensive research.

NEET Subject-wise Weightage

Before diving into books, understand the marks distribution: Biology carries 360 marks (180 questions), Physics carries 180 marks (45 questions), and Chemistry carries 180 marks (45 questions). This means Biology is the most critical subject, contributing 50% of the total marks.

Best Books for NEET Biology

Biology is where NEET is won or lost. Here is the complete reading list:

  • NCERT Biology Class 11 & 12 (Absolutely Mandatory) — Over 80% of NEET Biology questions come directly from NCERT text, diagrams, and tables. Read every single line. Memorize all diagrams, all tabular data, and every exception mentioned in NCERT. This is the single most important activity for NEET Biology.
  • Trueman's Objective Biology for NEET — The most widely used supplementary book for NEET Biology. Contains MCQs organized exactly as NCERT chapters, with detailed explanations for each answer. Excellent for testing your NCERT knowledge and filling gaps.
  • MTG Fingertips Biology — Best for quick revision. Contains chapter-wise notes and a large MCQ bank. The 'last minute revision' features are very helpful in the weeks before the exam.
  • Pradeep Biology Class 11 & 12 — More detailed than NCERT, useful for topics like Cell Biology and Genetics where NCERT leaves some gaps. Reference only — do not try to memorize everything from Pradeep.

Best Books for NEET Physics

Physics in NEET tests fundamental concepts and numerical problem-solving. Questions are at a slightly lower difficulty than JEE Main but require solid conceptual understanding.

  • NCERT Physics Class 11 & 12 — As with Biology, NCERT Physics is the foundation. Read all derivations, all examples, and solve all exercises.
  • DC Pandey Objective Physics for NEET (Arihant) — Specifically designed for NEET Physics. Excellent MCQ bank organized topicwise with solutions. DC Pandey understands exactly what NEET tests and calibrates the problems accordingly.
  • H.C. Verma Concepts of Physics — Reading the theory sections from HC Verma is very helpful for building strong concepts. The exercises are more advanced than NEET but the understanding they build gives you an edge.

Best Books for NEET Chemistry

  • NCERT Chemistry Class 11 & 12 (Most Important) — Physical and Inorganic Chemistry in NEET is almost entirely NCERT. Read every reaction, every equation, every definition. For Organic, understand mechanisms rather than memorising.
  • OP Tandon Organic Chemistry — For deeper understanding of Organic Chemistry mechanisms and reactions. Supplement with NCERT for reaction lists.
  • MTG Fingertips Chemistry — Quick revision notes and MCQ bank for all three parts of Chemistry.

NEET Previous Year Papers

Solving 15 years of previous NEET papers is the single most effective preparation activity after completing your syllabus. NEET questions often repeat concepts (though not exactly), and pattern recognition gives a huge advantage. MTG's 33 Years NEET Chapterwise book is the most comprehensive collection.
