Study Tips for Class 10 & 12 Board Exams — How to Score 90+

Board exams are a critical milestone in every student's academic journey. Class 10 board results determine which stream you enter in Class 11, while Class 12 marks are used for college admissions and form the basis for cutoffs at many premier institutions. With so much riding on these exams, having an effective preparation strategy is essential.

This comprehensive guide presents evidence-based study tips, time management strategies, and subject-wise advice that have helped thousands of students score 90+ marks in CBSE board exams.

Start Early — The Most Important Tip

Students who score 90+ in board exams typically start their preparation 4–6 months before the exam. Starting early gives you time to complete the syllabus without panic, revise multiple times, practise from sample papers, and handle any weak areas before they become problems during the exam.

Build a Realistic Study Schedule

A study schedule that you actually follow is worth more than a perfect schedule that sits on paper. Here is how to build one that works: Divide your day into study blocks of 45–50 minutes with 10-minute breaks. Allocate more time to subjects you find difficult. Ensure you have at least 2–3 hours of free time daily for rest, hobbies, and family. Review your schedule every week and adjust based on progress.

Subject-wise Tips for Class 10 Boards

Mathematics

  • Solve all NCERT exercises and examples — many board questions are direct or slight variations of NCERT problems
  • Practise at least 15–20 problems daily to maintain calculation speed
  • Show all steps in your working — even if you make a calculation error, you get marks for correct method
  • Identify your weak chapters early and dedicate extra time to them

Science

  • Draw diagrams for every chapter — electric circuits, plant diagrams, human body systems etc.
  • Learn definitions precisely — examiners look for specific keywords
  • Balance all chemical equations — unbalanced equations lose marks
  • Connect concepts to daily life examples for better retention

Social Science

  • Create chapter-wise notes with headings, subheadings, and key points
  • For History and Political Science, practice writing 3-mark and 5-mark answers in the correct format
  • For Geography, practice map-based questions and identify all important locations on the map
  • Read newspaper headlines to connect current events with syllabus topics

The Power of Previous Year Papers

Solving the last 10 years of CBSE board papers is one of the most effective preparation techniques. It shows you exactly what type of questions are asked, which chapters carry more marks, and how to format your answers for maximum marks. CBSE makes its previous year papers freely available on their website, and Academic Halt also provides links to important sample papers.

Revision Strategy — The Key to Retention

According to memory research, information is best retained when revised at increasing intervals: revise a chapter after 1 day, then after 3 days, then after 1 week, then after 1 month. This spaced repetition approach ensures long-term retention. Create one-page chapter summaries for quick revision in the weeks before the exam.

Health and Wellness During Board Preparation

Many students sacrifice sleep and exercise during board preparation, which actually reduces their performance. Aim for 7–8 hours of sleep. Short 20-minute daily exercise improves concentration by 20–30%. Take one half-day off per week completely. Eat nutritious meals — brain function depends heavily on nutrition.
